# Pigeon Seed Mix Research Notes

## General Nutritional Requirements

### Macronutrient Ratios
- **Protein**: 13.5-15% (standard), up to 17.5% for racing/breeding
- **Carbohydrates**: 60-70%
- **Fat**: 2-5%
- **Fiber**: Under 5% (pigeons don't utilize fiber well)

### Key Ingredient Categories

#### 1. Grains (50-70% of mix)
- **Corn** (yellow preferred - source of Vitamin A)
  - Primary energy source
  - Basis of all pigeon diets
  - White corn should be avoided (deficient in Vitamin A)
- **Wheat** (hard red winter wheat: 13.5% protein, soft red: 10.2%)
  - Higher protein than corn/milo
  - May improve fertility
- **Milo/Grain Sorghum**
  - Similar to corn but harder
  - Lacks Vitamin A but good with corn
- **Barley**
- **Oats/Oat Groats**

#### 2. Legumes/Protein Sources (15-20% of mix)
- **Peas** (most essential grain)
  - Austrian winter peas
  - Canada peas
  - Field peas
  - High in protein and vitamins
  - Excellent for squab development
- **Lentils**
- **Beans** (must be cooked except peas, lentils, mung beans)
- **Mung beans** (safe uncooked)

#### 3. Seeds (10-15% of mix)
- **Safflower seed**
- **Millet** (white proso millet)
- **Linseed/Flax**
- **Canola**
- **Black oil sunflower seeds**

## Mix Ratios by Purpose

### Standard Mix (13.5% protein)
- Grains: 60-70%
- Legumes: 15-20%
- Seeds: 10-15%

### Racing/Breeding Mix (17.5% protein)
- Higher pea content (up to 50% various peas)
- Carbohydrates: ~62%
- Calories: ~359 kcal/100g

### General Rule from Facebook Groups
- Grains/seeds: 1/3
- Nuts: 1/3
- Legumes: 1/3

## Special Considerations

### Different Pigeon Situations
- **Racing**: Higher protein (17.5%), more peas
- **Breeding**: 15% protein, balanced mix
- **Molting**: Higher protein needs
- **Winter**: Higher fat/energy needs
- **Broody**: Standard maintenance

## Important Notes
- Pigeons do poorly on mash
- Green feed not necessary
- Yellow corn essential for Vitamin A
- Peas are the most essential ingredient
- Hard grains like milo are acceptable
- Fiber should be minimized


## Seasonal and Situational Adjustments

### Molting Season
- **Protein**: Minimum 16% (higher than standard)
- **Key nutrients**: Sulphur amino acids (methionine, cystine)
- **Feather composition**: 86% keratin (mineralized protein)
- **Special considerations**: 
  - Avoid medication unless emergency
  - Provide bathing opportunities 1-2x per week
  - Add brewer's yeast for amino acids and B vitamins
  - Black maize rich in antioxidants for immunity
  - Extra energy needed for feather renewal

### Winter Season
- **Energy needs**: Up to 30% more energy for body heat
- **Focus**: High carbohydrates and fats, moderate protein
- **Recommended winter mix**:
  - 50% Barley (easily digestible energy)
  - 20% Maize/Corn (high calories, body heat)
  - 10% Wheat (bulk, moderate energy)
  - 10% Peas (protein for maintenance)
  - 5% Safflower (high fat, omega oils)
  - 5% Linseed/Flaxseed (feather health)
- **Oil seeds**: Add 5-10% hemp, sunflower hearts, rapeseed
- **Supplements**: Garlic oil, apple cider vinegar, probiotics, cod liver oil (vitamin D)
- **Feeding**: 30-40g per bird per day, twice daily
- **Avoid**: Overfeeding protein, moldy grain, corn-only diets

### Breeding/Brooding Season
- **Protein**: 15% (standard breeder mix)
- **Special additions**: 
  - Flaxseed oil coating
  - Whey protein (avoid chocolate - toxic)
  - Balanced nutrition for egg production and squab growth
- **Allow natural behavior**: Rest, pairing, breeding

### Racing Season
- **Protein**: 17.5% (high performance)
- **Peas**: Up to 50% for races over 300 miles
- **Energy**: Higher carbohydrates (62%)
- **Calories**: ~359 kcal/100g
- **Special mixes for long races**: 
  - 20% maize, 15% kaffir corn, 15% hemp
  - 15% safflower, 20% conditioning feed, 15% other

### Rest/Maintenance
- **Protein**: 13.5-15% (standard)
- **Focus**: Balanced nutrition, natural behavior
- **Light feeding**: Depurative mixes with barley, milo, low-protein grains
